Introduction

What is Health & Well-Being?

Definition

Health is a state of complete physical, mental, and social well-being — not just the absence of disease. Well-being includes emotional, psychological, and social wellness.

Why It Matters Today

In the 21st century, health challenges are global. Pandemics, climate change, and social inequalities affect everyone. Understanding well-being helps us build a better future.

Key Insight

The WHO constitution states: "Health is a fundamental human right." Yet, billions of people still lack access to basic healthcare services.

Historical Connection

Health & The Holocaust

The Holocaust (1939–1945) represents one of the darkest chapters in history, where systematic destruction of health and well-being was used as a weapon of oppression and genocide.

Loss of Basic Rights

Millions were denied food, shelter, medicine, and all conditions necessary for health.

Medical Ethics Violations

Nazi doctors performed cruel experiments on prisoners, leading to modern medical ethics codes.

Legacy & Lessons

The Nuremberg Code was created to ensure informed consent in medical practice — a direct result.

Lesson: Health and well-being must be protected as human rights. History shows that when they are violated, the consequences are catastrophic for all of humanity.
